008 : Business Case - Aerofitüî©

üåü Overview: (Scaler Casestudy)
The market research team at AeroFit is seeking to enhance customer recommendations for their range of treadmills by identifying distinct characteristics within their target audience. The objective is to create detailed customer profiles for each AeroFit treadmill product, leveraging descriptive analytics and contingency tables. By exploring differences in customer characteristics across products, the team aims to provide more personalized recommendations to new customers.

üî©About Aerofit:
Aerofit is a fitness equipment or exercise equipment brand that specializes in manufacturing and providing products related to aerobics and fitness. These products may include treadmills, elliptical trainers, exercise bikes, and other fitness machines designed to help individuals achieve their health and fitness goals.

üåê Key Highlights::
1.Dataset Overview: The dataset contains information about customers who purchased different AeroFit treadmill products ("KP281," "KP481," and "KP781"). Features include customer demographics (age, gender, education, marital status), product usage details (frequency, fitness rating), and financial information (income, expected miles).

2.Data Quality and Types: The dataset comprises 180 entries with no missing values. Data types include integers for numerical attributes and objects for categorical attributes. Notable numerical attributes: Age, Education, Usage, Fitness, Income, Miles.

3.Descriptive Statistics: Descriptive statistics provide insights into the central tendency, spread, and distribution of numerical attributes. Age has a right-skewed distribution, with some older customers. Usage and Fitness show variations, suggesting diverse customer behaviors. Income exhibits a right-skewed distribution, indicating varying income levels. Miles expected per week vary, with some customers planning to cover longer distances.

4.Product Distribution: "KP281" is the most popular treadmill, followed by "KP481" and "KP781." Insights into product popularity can guide marketing strategies and inventory management.

5.Cluster Analysis: Customer segmentation using K-means clustering identifies four distinct clusters based on age, income, and fitness. Pair plots visually represent the differences between clusters, aiding in targeted marketing.

6.Conditional and Marginal Probabilities: Conditional probabilities, e.g., the probability of a male customer buying the "KP781" treadmill, provide insights into specific customer behaviors. Marginal probabilities indicate the overall distribution of product purchases, helping in market targeting.

7.Correlation Analysis: Correlation matrix and heatmap reveal relationships between numerical attributes. Pair plots showcase relationships between attributes based on the purchased product.

8.Recommendations:
- Tailor marketing strategies based on age and gender demographics.
- Consider promotions for less popular products to boost sales.
- Evaluate pricing and features for customer segments with lower incomes.
- Enhance customer engagement based on usage, fitness goals, and expected mileage.
- Explore income-based services or loyalty programs to cater to diverse customer needs.

9.Continuous Improvement: Implement customer feedback mechanisms for ongoing product and service enhancements. Stay competitive through market research, competitor benchmarking, and data-driven decision-making.

10.Business Strategy: Formulate a comprehensive business strategy encompassing product diversification, customer education, and effective customer support. Foster a culture of data-driven decision-making for sustained business success.
