üåü Overview: (Scaler Casestudy)
The business problem at hand revolves around understanding and analyzing customer purchase behavior, with a specific focus on purchase amounts during Black Friday at Walmart Inc. The primary objective is to discern potential variations in spending habits between male and female customers. With an assumed customer base of 50 million males and 50 million females, the management team aims to derive insights that can inform strategic decision-making.

üîÜAbout Walmart:
Walmart Inc founded by Sam Walton in 1962, is a global retail giant headquartered in Bentonville, Arkansas. Operating across multiple segments, including Walmart U.S, Walmart International, and Sam's Club, the company is renowned for its extensive range of products and services. With a significant presence in e-commerce through Walmart.com, it continues to be a key player in the retail industry.

1.Dataset Overview: The dataset comprises 325,565 entries with unique User_IDs ranging from 1,000,001 to 1,006,040. Key columns include User_ID, Product_ID, Gender, Age, Occupation, City_Category, Stay_In_Current_City_Years, Marital_Status, Product_Category, and Purchase.

2.Customer Demographics: Gender distribution shows 245,458 male and 80,108 female customers. Age information is available but not displayed, necessitating further exploration.

2.Occupation and Marital Status: Occupations vary (mean ‚âà 8.08), with most falling between codes 2 to 14. Marital status is binary (0 for unmarried, 1 for married), with approximately 41% married customers.

3.Product Categories and Purchase Amount: Products are categorized into 18 groups; the average product category purchased is around 5.29. Average purchase amount is $9,324.12, ranging from $185 to $23,961, with a right-skewed distribution.

4.Insights: Dataset includes diverse customers, skewed towards unmarried individuals. Occupations and product categories show a wide range, indicating varied preferences. Right-skewed purchase amounts suggest occasional high-value purchases.

5.Null Values and Outliers: Null values exist in various columns, with one missing entry each in Occupation, City_Category, Stay_In_Current_City_Years, Marital_Status, Product_Category, and Purchase. Outliers in the 'Purchase' column are visible through boxplot analysis.

6.Gender-Based Spending Analysis: Average spending per transaction for male customers is $9,492.26, higher than the $8,808.90 for female customers.

7.Inference: On average, male customers tend to spend more per transaction.
